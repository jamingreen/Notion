The Data Protection Act 1998

  • Applies to information stored on a computer and in organised paper filter system.
  • Covers personal data (any data that can be used to identify a living person)
    • Including a combination of data that can used to identify a living person
  • Have been replaced by General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018

Principles

  1. Processed fairly and lawfully
  1. Only obtain for one or more specified and lawful purpose
  1. Data should be adequate, relevant and not excessive for its purpose
  1. Accurate and kept up to date
  1. Not kept for longer than is necessary for its purpose
  1. Process in accordance with the right of data subjects under this Act
  1. Appropriate technical and organisational measures shall be taken against unauthorised or unlawful processing of personal data and against accidental loss or destruction of, or damage to, personal data
  1. Personal shall not be transferred to a country or territory outside of European Economic Area, unless that country or territory ensures adequate level of data protection
  • Right of data subjects
    • Right to request a copy of the data held about them
    • Right to correct the data held about them
    • Right to prevent marketing using contact details provided by the data subject

Disadvantages

  • Difficult to enforce
  • Require extra administration and expense
  • The last principle of the Act is impossible and impractical to enforce

The Computer Misuse Act 1990

  • Concerns the malicious use of computers
  • Frequently update to ensures it remain relevant

Primary Offences

  1. Unauthorised access to computer materials
    • Find a teacher’s password and opening their files
  1. Unauthorised access with intent to commit further offences
    • Find a teacher’s password and opening their files with the intention of increasing your mark.
  1. Unauthorised modification of computer material
    • Find a teacher’s password and opening their files with the intention of increasing your mark.
  1. Making, supplying or obtaining anything which can be used in computer misuse offences

The Copyright, Design and Patents Act 1988

  • Protect people’s property online
    • Books, music, video and software
  • If the work is original, copyright automatically applies and doesn’t expires until 25-70 years after the death of creator
  • Include computer programs as a type of literary work
  • If an individuals believes that their work has been copied, it is their responsibility to take action

  • How to defend copyright
    • The user must enter a unique key before the software is installed
    • Some software will only run if the CD is present in the drive
    • Some applications will only run if a special piece of hardware called a ‘dongle’ is plugged into the USB port on the computer

Algorithm

Cannot be copyrighted as it is a idea

But if you implement the algorithm in software, it can be copyrighted

The idea cannot be copyrighted but the detail implementation of the idea can be copyrighted

The Regulation of Investigatory Power Act 2000

  • Investigation, surveillance and interception of communication by public bodies
    • Enables certain public bodies to demand that someone hand over keys to protected information
  • Need to have a warrant
  • Enables mass surveillance of communications in transit and monitoring of Internet activities
  • Enforced internet service providers and mobile phone companies to give up information upon request.
  • Ensure internet service providers and mobile phone companies that they have sufficient hardware installed to facilitate surveillance

  • Controversial
    • Its power extends to small agencies
    • Invasion of privacy
    • Often improperly used
